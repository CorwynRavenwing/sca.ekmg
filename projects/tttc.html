Tailor To The Crown Awards

The "Tailor (or Seamstress) to the Crown" award is given by Their Majesties to a gentle who has been involved in creating garb for Their Majesties. Some Royals choose to call the award "Artisan to the Crown" and give it to those who have created other gifts as well.

In either case, rather than giving the recipient a scroll to commemorate the award, in recent reigns the Royals have given a small item of jewelry, which the Metalsmiths' Guild has been honored to provide to Them for this purpose. This allows the recipient to wear their award on their person, as well as takes some of the burden off of the Kingdom's Calligraphers, who would otherwise be called upon to create a large number of awards scrolls for this purpose.

Tailor To The Crown #10

 This project was performed for Their Majesties Andreas III & Gabriella I. It is in the form of a bronze ring, marked with Their Magesties' initials. The (original) waxes were carved by hand. From these originals, a silicone negative mold was created, more (duplicate) positive waxes were cast from the silicone mold, and these were treed up and cast in Nu Gold (a new bronze alloy). The "stone" in each ring is of jeweller's cold enamel, with colors chosen to resemble ruby, onyx, garnet, and light blue sapphire.
