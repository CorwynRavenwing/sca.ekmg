Metalsmiths' Symposium (MSS)

This page is about the series of events known as the "Metalsmiths' Symposium". If you have a suggestion of something that should be added to this page, please contact the Webminister at webminister@ekmg.eastkingdom.org.

(BACK)

The Metalsmiths' Symposium is a weekend event dedicated to the metal arts - all of them! Blacksmithing, whitesmithing, cold forging, casting, jewelry and lapidary work, glasswork of all kinds (lampworking, blown glass, and stained glass to name a few), plus other arts like tool making and sharpening and shop safety. Also included are dyeing, herbalism, candlemaking, woodworking, pottery, perfumery, calligraphy and illumination, embroidery....if it involves metal, fire, or tools, MSS tries to have a class on it!

Even though the MSS web pages are on the EKMG website, the Guild is not "in charge" of the MSS's. We volunteer at them, have lots of fun at them, support them by teaching and encouraging others to come and teach and take classes, and instigate lots of evil metal fun at them, but the Autocrat of the event comes from the hosting Barony / Shire. We figured that having a "central location" for MSS information helps people find it, especially people from other Kingdoms - there's only one URL to bookmark for all the MSS's. Come and enjoy and have fun with us!

    Attendee Information (So, You Want to Come to an MSS?)

    What to bring:

    1. Garb made of natural fibers - linen, leather, wool, cotton - that you don't mind getting dirty, burned, and wet all at once. Synthetic fibers melt, while natural fibers only char, so they're the best things to wear at fiery classes.
    2. Your tool kit(s), if you have them - but please mark the kits with your badge, maker's mark, or initials to prevent tool confusion! It's even a good idea to mark your tools (a Dremel is good for this, but the paint pens sold at craft stores work well too).
    3. Safety glasses - if you have them, bring them. If you don't, you should have them.
    4. Garb for all seasons - most of the MSS's have been cold weather, so bring layers of garb, and your cloak!
    5. Basic feast gear - plate, cup, bowl, spoon, fork, knife, napkin. You don't want your good gear to get grungy from the classes, so pack basic and light (if you have them - if not, we'll try to be careful! ;-) )
    6. Bring a cup, goblet, or mug you can keep with you all day - ya gotta keep hydrated, even if it is cold as...um...blazes?
    7. Cold-weather bedding (I bring two thick blankets, as well as sheets, pillow, *and* I throw my cloak on top!) There are heated cabins, so no beds and tents are needed unless you really want to.
    8. Check the requirements of the classes you want to take, and if you can, bring the requested tools / supplies

    Teaching and Taking Classes at MSS

    We have created a class scheduling tool that allows people to sign up for teaching classes and have them automatically show up on the website as a planned class. In the future we plan to add being able to edit or cancel your own classes, as well as doing the hourly class schedule on-line. This tool isn't just for Metalsmiths' Symposia, but can be used for any event at which there is going to be a Guild track of classes. Contact the webminister at to set up an event as being on the scheduler.

    • History of MSS

    It's all his fault! Olaf of Trollhelmsford is the gentle who originated the idea of a Metal Smiths' Symposium back in 2002, after we were talking on the East Kingdom Metalsmiths email list about getting together at an event and doing a metal track of classes. That led to the idea of having an event that was entirely about metalwork, and Olaf was the one who got a site and enough teachers to make it happen!

    Lavena Knappe in AEthelmearc took up the MSS challenge the next year, after talking at length with Olaf about setup and ideas. That site was on a cliff overlooking Lake Ontario, with a large retaining wall to prevent mishaps on the edge...

    Carowyn Silveroak and Corwyn Ravenwing then thought about taking it to the corner of three Kingdoms, to let some other Kingdoms have fun too! Two shires worked together to share the fun!

    Haraldr Bassi had such fun at that one that he decided to bring the fun to his own shire - and Tropical Storm Tammy couldn't stop us from continuing it!
